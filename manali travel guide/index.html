About Manali

Manali is a town, near Kullu town in Kullu district in the Indian state of Himachal Pradesh.[2] It is situated in the northern end of the Kullu Valley, formed by the Beas River. The town is located in the Kullu district.Manali is named after the Sanātanī lawgiver Manu.Manu is believed to have stepped off his ark in Manali to recreate human life after a great flood had deluged the world.The Kullu Valley in which Manali is situated is often referred to as the "Valley of the Gods". An old village in the town has an ancient temple dedicated to the sage Manu.Near by Places are Hidimba Devi Temple, Dhungri Van Vihar, Club House, Vashisht Kund (Baths), Solang Valley, Gulaba, Bijli Mahadev Temple, Atal Tunnel, Sissu Waterfall, koksar, Manikaran Gurudwara, Naggar Castle, Jogini Waterfal.

Activities in Solang valley:

Paragliding

paragliding in Solang Valley will give you a sense of freedom like you have never experienced before.

Skiing

Manali is known for its excellent skiing conditions with skiing enthusiasts visiting from all over the country. The undulating slopes of Solang Valley and Rohtang Pass are perfect for skiing.

Zorbing

Zorbing is a fun hill-sport in this region that takes advantage of the picturesque downward slopes of the Himalayas.

Quad Biking

Summer in the valleys offers some exciting quad biking experiences.
